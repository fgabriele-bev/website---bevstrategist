About

Over three decades in the hospitality industry, of which the last one as Group Beverages Director across a collection of UK hotels. In that time I have been the person translating a wine list and a drinks menu into something a guest, a team, and a business all had reason to believe in — evaluating producers, listing distributors, building programmes, and training teams to understand not just what was on the list, but why, so the promise on paper and the experience at the table were the same thing.

That role was never about negotiating the best price. It was about deciding what belonged on the list and why — whether a producer's story would land with a guest, whether a team could sell it with confidence, whether a category made commercial sense for that property at that moment. The decision always sat at the intersection of product, people, and purpose, never in isolation.

That experience is the practice — a method built on years of conversation and listening, with guests, teams, colleagues, distributors, brand ambassadors, and producers. It is a direct understanding of how hospitality buyers decide, what producers need to communicate, and what it takes to close the gap between product potential and operational reality.

That vantage point works both ways. For producers, it means a direct, current understanding of how UK buyers actually decide — not something inferred from another market. For hospitality operators, it means someone who has run the function from inside a group, and knows precisely where a well-written list and a well-delivered service can drift apart.
